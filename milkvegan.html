Claim: Can't you just milk an animal and it not be unethical

Details

The year is 2505. Everyone is vegan. We have a sanctuary with some cows that aren't forcibly interacted with. What's wrong with milking one and selling the milk?

Problems with this Argument

1. Cows only produce milk when pregnant

So without artificial insemination, you're relying on the natural reproductive cycle of cows to determine when to drink their milk, which the calf is cutting into, and the cow only is producing significant milk for a relatively short period of its life, maybe a quarter - if you're lucky.

2. Help me out here

Ok, so here's the plan, you now need to keep cows for at least 4 times their current lifespan well past when they make milk, you have at least twice as many animals (males are now not automatically slaughtered). So you're looking at at least an order of magnitude reduction in the amount of milk right off the bat. If you're looking at grass-fed only and you're not artifically inseminating the cows to produce the milk (because you've somehow reasoned yourself out of beastiality), it's even worse on the sustainability. And with no slaughter market to keep such milk profitable, there will be orders of magnitude less animals (for reference, there are about 30000 total American Bison alive today, which is how many cows are slaughtered in America on average every 8 hours).

So lets do this calculation, shall we? Let's just talk US, and be generous and assume 100000 cows, so at let's say even a lifespan of 10 years to be more generous (so that's 10000 pregnancies per year). And with all this generousity you're looking at a cow pregnancy rate (and therefore milk production rate) of slightly less than 0.03% our current rate, assuming we avoid practices like mother-calf separation or forced milking, etc. because, again, I'm just so generous.

So let me get this straight, your big-brain plan is to avoid going 100% vegan by going 99.97% vegan. Okay. Great job. Well done.

3. This is a fantasy anyway

We're now pretty deep into fantasy land here; this alternate universe has nothing to do with reality. You can't use this as any sort of justification for using animal products in the real world, where it involves the active torture and slaughter of animals.
