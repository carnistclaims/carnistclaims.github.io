Claim: Morality is subjective anyways

Details

The claim here is that was can axiomatially define morality to include carnism

Problems with this Argument

1. It's not morality then

Call it schmorality, or whatever you want. But unless it focuses on the reduction of needless harm towards the wellbeing of conscious beings, it can't be morality. So I can axiomatically define moral as 'giving the author of carnist.cc free money' and the opposite immoral, but is that really actually morality?

2. You really pretzel up

If you want your morality to be internally consistent, you have to take into account the human impact of eating animals. So now it starts to become ok and not ok to do highly arbitrary combinations of things.

3. Just admit it already

You're inflicting needless suffering and reducing the wellbeing of conscious creatures for your own entertainment when you eat meat. There's not a good justification for this in any reasonable moral system.
